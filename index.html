Summary of the Video

This video introduces the basic operation of a four‑stroke internal combustion engine. It explains how the intake, compression, power, and exhaust strokes work together to convert fuel into motion. Students see how pistons, valves, spark plugs, and the crankshaft interact, and they are introduced to concepts like displacement, torque, and horsepower. It’s a clear, visual foundation for understanding engine mechanics.

Knowledge & Understanding (Answers)

  1. What are the four strokes of the engine cycle?

    Intake, Compression, Power, and Exhaust

  2. Why is valve timing important?

    It ensures valves open and close at the right time for efficient combustion.

  3. What part ignites the air‑fuel mixture?

    The spark plug.

  4. What does the crankshaft do??

    Converts piston motion into rotation.

  5. Why is compression necessary?

    It increases the energy released during combustion.
