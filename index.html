How To Identify Bolts (US Standard and Metric)

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-_8d1h3AnP0

Summary of the Video

This video explains how to identify bolts by size, thread type, and measurement system. It demonstrates the differences between US Standard (SAE) and Metric bolts, how to read bolt markings, how to measure diameter and thread pitch, and how to match bolts to the correct nuts. The instructor shows real examples and uses simple tools such as thread gauges and calipers to help viewers understand how to correctly identify and sort fasteners.

Knowledge & Understanding

  7. Why is it important to match a bolt to the correct nut?
  8. What does the length of a bolt measure?
  9. What happens if you force a metric bolt into an SAE nut?
  10. Why is proper bolt identification important in mechanical work?

Knowledge & Understanding (Answers)

  1. What is the difference between US Standard (SAE) and Metric bolts?

    SAE bolts use inches for measurement, while Metric bolts use millimeters.

  2. What tool can be used to measure bolt diameter?

    A caliper or bolt gauge.

  3. What does thread pitch refer to?

    The distance between threads (metric) or the number of threads per inch (SAE).

  4. Why are bolt head markings important?

    They indicate bolt grade, strength, and sometimes the measurement system.

  5. How can you tell if a bolt is metric or SAE by its markings?

    Metric bolts often have numbers like 8.8 or 10.9, while SAE bolts use lines or grade markings.

  6. What is the purpose of a thread gauge?

    To determine the correct thread pitch or threads per inch.

  7. Why is it important to match a bolt to the correct nut?

    Mismatched threads can strip, bind, or fail under load.

  8. What does the length of a bolt measure?

    The distance from the underside of the head to the end of the bolt.

  9. What happens if you force a metric bolt into an SAE nut?

    It can damage the threads and create a dangerous mechanical failure.

  10. Why is proper bolt identification important in mechanical work?

    It ensures correct fit, strength, and safety in assemblies.
